ABOUT THE PROJECT

BODYMORPHOSIS

Project Overview

BodyMorphosis, a collaborative exhibition of interactive 3D art pieces by Minnie Park & Adrian Frichitthavong, unveils a dialogue between art and technology at the BlackBox. This interactive exhibition delves into the convergence of augmented reality and digital environments, provoking reflection on our evolving human experience. AR facial sculptures, inspired by raw human emotion, serve as an opportunity to reflect on our feelings and inner selves. Complementing this, interactive virtual environments, moulded by the omnipresence of AI, confront the viewer with the pressing concerns surrounding its societal impact. Softwares used include TouchDesigner, UnrealEngine5, Metaspark and Blender. BodyMorphoses prompts introspection through audiovisual, interactive and immersive digital environment experiences.

QUANTUM FACES

by MINNIE PARK

PROJECT DESCRIPTION

Humans rely on the memories they have accumulated since birth and react accordingly to these memories. How they think, their perspectives, responses, and the emotions they experience are calculated and computed by neurons formed through past memories. All these memories are gathered to form their thoughts and the system of emotions, which becomes their identity. Humans' thoughts cannot surpass their emotions. Therefore, emotions in humans are the measure of all things.

Emotion, from the perspective of quantum physics regarding the electromagnetic field inside the brain, is a form of energy, which means it is also a frequency (Idris, 2020). When humans feel something, they emit energy that can affect others, which, in turn, can affect themselves.

Even when the given experience is the same, individuals react and feel differently. 'Quantum Faces' provides the experience of AR face filters exploring emotions. This allows the audience to explore and observe their feelings, contemplate their pre-formed system of thoughts and emotions, and consider the effects of giving and receiving emotions/energy on one another. The main question of the project is, 'If you had the choice to affect others either positively or negatively, what would you choose?'

Quantum Faces features 4 facial sculptures named with words created by the artist. They represent feelings that do not yet have words, undefined by language. By defining them and giving names to these feelings, it offers the opportunity to process these emotions and move forward, facilitating more effective communication between people.

“Reflethos”
The awareness that your mind is listening to what you are saying to yourself.

A moment of realisation that your mind is listening to your words. It's as if you are looking at your inner consciousness as a different person, standing and staring one step away from this person. It's the moment of becoming aware of how your words hold vast and limitless power, demolishing any stereotypes, prejudices, biases, and preconceptions. It’s the feeling of being overwhelmed by the power of your words to yourself.

What are the words you have said to yourself today?

“Facevoir”
The feeling of desiring to understand your facial expressions.

It is when you realise how little attention you pay to look at yourself in the mirror and observe your facial expressions to process what you are going through. It becomes an explorative and speculative journey, an experience you have never encountered before. Your face feels stranger than ever.

“Perspectrophe”
The awareness of how everyone looks at you differently.

It is believed that every individual perceives the world differently, considering it as a part of their own reality. The sense of awareness that others have varied perceptions of you implies the existence of different versions of yourself in this world.

Which version of yourself is the most authentic? Can any of them be considered your true self?

“Gazenopsia”
The feeling of thinking that this intense eye contact will become a powerful memory of that person.

The feeling of knowing that a particular eye contact with someone will become etched in your memory, much like a vivid picture, which will remain as one of the most powerful images whenever you think about that person. It's as if you will never forget the piercing eyes of your partner or the affectionate eyes of your mother.

What sort of gazenopsia would you want to evoke?





PHARMAKON AI

by ADRIAN FRICHITTHAVONG

PROJECT DESCRIPTION

Pharmakon_AI reflects on the ethical implications of posthumanist aspirations, as you take form as various AI advertisements scattered around Neo-Melbourne, a place where we co-exist with AI, humans and digital entities personalities have become indistinguishable from one another.

The built world provides a lens where AI has seamlessly integrated with a pro-capitalist society, advertising & consumerism become intertwined with our everyday lives. As we question whether it is beneficial or harmful for society to embrace a pro-AI stance to quell the epidemics that plague the modern world, we must also question who are the larger forces at play and who is really benefiting from who?
